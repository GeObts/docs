## The short version

1. Miners point their hardware at the pool.
2. The pool aggregates hashrate and submits work to the Bitcoin network.
3. When a block is found, the reward is split among contributors based on the work they did.
4. NFT holders on Base get their share of pool rewards through on-chain claims.
5. Everything is transparent: hashrate, fee, and payouts are published live.

That's it. The rest of this page is just detail.

## The model

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hashrate comes in">
    Hashrate enters the pool from three places:

    - **Home miners** with their own hardware
    - **NFT holders** whose mint contributes a backing share to the pool's operating capacity
    - **External hashpower markets** routed in to keep the pool competitive during low-participation windows
  </Step>
  <Step title="Work gets coordinated">
    The pool distributes mining work to all connected hashrate. Every share submitted is logged, attributed, and counted toward the contributor's payout.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Blocks get found">
    When a connected miner finds a valid block, the pool submits it to the Bitcoin network. The pool earns the block reward + fees.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rewards go out">
    Rewards are paid to:

    - Direct miners — to the BTC address registered on their worker
    - NFT holders — claimable on Base via the dashboard
  </Step>
</Steps>

  - Custody your hardware
  - Custody your BTC payouts (they go straight to your address)
  - Wrap your Bitcoin into a token
  - Charge a setup fee
  - Lock you into a contract
</Warning>

If you can plug in a miner, you can use BasedMining. If you can mint an NFT, you can use BasedMining.
